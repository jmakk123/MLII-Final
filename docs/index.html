02 · Data and Methodology

From raw WRDS to model-ready tensors

Two raw data sources, five processing stages, one 97-dimensional feature matrix aligned to 87,995 firm-year anchors. The target is forward drawdown — the maximum peak-to-trough loss in the 12 months following each firm's fiscal-year anchor date (filings date + 90 days). Values live in [−1, 0].

Why forward drawdown?

Bankruptcy prediction is too sparse — only 387 events in our universe. Raw return is too noisy — good companies can have bad years. Forward drawdown sits in the middle: a continuous, economically meaningful signal that captures firm-level risk without requiring the company to actually fail.

03 · Our Models and Process

Dual-stream fusion network

Structured financial history and recent price dynamics are fundamentally different signals — encoding them with different architectures before fusing is the core design choice. We tested two financial-branch candidates (LSTM vs. MLP-flatten) against three baselines.

Architecture (plain PyTorch)
┌─ Financial Branch ───────────────────┐
Input (batch, 5, 18)
Candidate A: LSTM 2-layer, h=64 → Linear → 32d
Candidate B: Flatten → MLP [128,64] → 32d
├─ Price Branch ───────────────────────┤
Input (batch, 7)
MLP 2-layer, h=32 → 16d
├─ Fusion Head ────────────────────────┤
LayerNorm(48d) → MLP [32] → scalar
Dropout 0.2 · Weight decay 1e-4
└─ Training ────────────────────────────┘
Huber loss δ=0.05 · AdamW lr=1e-3

04 · Findings / Analysis

Our Conclusion

The best model — a pure financial LSTM (no price branch) — beats the vol-only baseline on MAE, RMSE, and R², and absolutely murders it on Brier score (0.2665 vs 0.5732 — a 54% improvement). PR-AUC is 0.27pp shy of the vol-only floor, essentially a tie. The surprise finding: the financial-only branch outperforms the full dual-stream fusion, suggesting the price features added noise rather than signal on top of the LSTM accounting representation.

💀
Brier Score — Absolutely Murdered
Vol-only Brier of 0.573 is near-chance — min-max normalized volatility is a terrible probability calibrator. The financial LSTM produces genuinely calibrated crash probabilities.

Key Takeaway

The result is nuanced: the deep model adds real value on classification and calibration, but raw volatility remains a tough regression benchmark. This is economically sensible — for a risk manager deciding whether to hedge, a well-calibrated crash probability is more useful than a precise drawdown magnitude. The fusion model wins where it matters most in practice.

06 · Presentation

The story in six slides

Core narrative from our ML II final presentation — the problem, our approach, and what we found.

−30%
Slide 01 · The Problem
Predicting the Fall Before It Happens
Bankruptcy prediction is too rare to model. Raw returns are too noisy to use directly. Forward maximum drawdown is the signal in the middle — continuous, economically interpretable, and observable for every public company over any 12-month window.
52% of test-set firms experienced a drawdown worse than −30% in the 2020–2023 period. This is not a rare event.
Slide 02 · The Data
25 Years, Two Sources, One Tensor
Compustat annual fundamentals (fyear 1999–2025) linked to CRSP daily prices via the CCM linktable. For each firm-year anchor: a (5, 18) accounting tensor covering the prior five fiscal years, plus 7 price features from the trailing 252 trading days.
87,995 firm-years · 29M daily rows · train-only scaling to prevent leakage
Slide 03 · The Architecture
Two Streams, One Prediction
A 2-layer LSTM encodes the sequential accounting history (oldest → newest fiscal year). A parallel price MLP encodes the 7 market-regime features. Both streams are fused through a LayerNorm + MLP head that outputs a scalar drawdown prediction.
Financial branch: (batch, 5, 18) → 32d · Price branch: (batch, 7) → 16d · Fusion: 48d → scalar
Slide 04 · The Result
Beats Vol-Only — With a Surprise
The financial-only LSTM (no price branch) is the best model: MAE 0.1444 vs 0.1532, R² 0.293 vs 0.166, and Brier score cut by 54% (0.267 vs 0.573). PR-AUC is a statistical tie (0.8662 vs 0.8689). The full dual-stream fusion actually underperforms — the price branch added noise, not signal.
✓ MAE −5.8% · ✓ R² +76% · ✓ Brier −54% · ~ PR-AUC (tie)  |  Fin-only > Full fusion
Slide 05 · Why It Matters
From Signal to Decision
A well-calibrated drawdown probability is directly usable: credit desks can trigger watchlists, hedge funds can rank short books, VCs can screen comparable public companies, and asset managers can size protective hedges. The signal is updated annually at each 10-K anchor date.
The 96%+ top-decile precision means: 9 out of 10 firms we flag as highest-risk are actually in the worst-realized decile that year.
Slide 06 · Limitations & Next Steps
Honest Assessment
The LSTM doesn't crush MAE because raw volatility is already near-optimal for regression. Future work: sector-conditioned heads, macro regime features (yield curve, VIX), and quarterly filing cadence instead of annual anchors to get sharper predictions at the firm level.
The model is most useful as a risk screener, not a precise return predictor.
