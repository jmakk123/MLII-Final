The Signal

Why forward drawdown?

Bankruptcy prediction is too sparse — only 387 events in our universe. Raw return is too noisy — good companies can have bad years and vice versa. Forward drawdown sits in the middle: a continuous, economically meaningful signal that captures firm-level risk without requiring the company to actually fail.

Our target: the maximum peak-to-trough loss a stock experiences in the 12 months following each fiscal year's anchor date (filings date + 90 days). Values are in [−1, 0].

Non-positive target ∈ [−1, 0] Anchor = datadate + 90d 12-month forward window Delistings → −1.0

Architecture & Results

Dual-stream fusion network

Structured financial history and recent price dynamics are structurally different signals — encoding them with different architectures then fusing them is the core novelty.

Practical Applications

Who uses a drawdown signal?

A forward drawdown prediction is immediately actionable across four distinct financial contexts — each with a different risk horizon and decision type.

🏦
Credit & Lending
Banks and credit desks incorporate the drawdown signal into loan covenant triggers and credit line reviews. A rising predicted drawdown for a borrower prompts earlier watchlist escalation — before financial statements deteriorate.
"Flag any borrower whose drawdown score worsens by >15pp quarter-over-quarter for immediate credit review."
🚀
Venture Capital
VCs considering follow-on rounds into public-company spinouts or pre-IPO comparables use the signal to gauge market-based distress risk. High predicted drawdown at the Series B/C stage is an early warning flag.
"Screen follow-on candidates in the bottom-two drawdown deciles before committing the next tranche."
📊
Long / Short Equity
Rank the investable universe each quarter by drawdown signal. Short the top decile (worst predicted), long the bottom decile. Our top-decile precision of 96%+ means short-side selection is highly accurate.
"Rotate shorts quarterly using worst-predicted-drawdown decile; rebalance in sync with 10-K anchor dates."
🛡️
Portfolio Insurance
Asset managers use drawdown scores to dynamically size hedges — put options or VIX exposure — on specific portfolio positions. Firms with high predicted drawdown get more protection allocated.
"Allocate put-option budget proportionally to each holding's drawdown signal; rebalance at earnings season."
